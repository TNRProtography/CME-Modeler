Spot The Aurora

Privacy Policy

Effective date: 27 March 2026  ·  TNR Protography

This policy explains what data Spot The Aurora collects, why we collect it, and how we protect it. We keep it plain and readable because privacy policies should actually make sense.

1. Who We Are

Spot The Aurora is a New Zealand-based aurora forecasting and space weather monitoring service operated by TNR Protography. We provide real-time aurora forecasts, solar wind data, CME tracking, and space weather alerts for New Zealand and the Southern Hemisphere.

Contact: help@spottheaurora.co.nz

2. What Data We Collect

2.1 Location Data

When you use Spot The Aurora, your device may ask permission to share your location. We use this to:

  • Provide localised aurora forecasts for your area
  • Show cloud cover and weather conditions near you
  • Calculate your distance from the aurora oval
  • Determine your local darkness window for aurora visibility
  • Place a pin on the aurora sighting map when you submit a report
  • Identify your nearest town to label your sighting report accurately

Your location is used on your device for forecasts and alerts. When you submit a sighting report, your location is stored to determine your nearest town and map pin position. If you enable push notifications, your location is stored to send you relevant local alerts. We do not use your location for advertising or share it with third parties.

2.2 Push Notification Tokens

If you enable aurora alerts, your device is assigned a push notification token. We use this token solely to send you space weather alerts when aurora activity is detected for New Zealand. We do not share notification tokens with third parties. You can disable notifications at any time through your device settings.

2.3 Aurora Sighting Reports

If you voluntarily submit a sighting report, we store:

  • Approximate location (town and region level only, not precise GPS coordinates)
  • Sighting status (e.g. visible, cloudy, nothing seen)
  • Timestamp of the report
  • Optional display name if you choose to provide one

Sighting reports are displayed on a shared map to help other users understand real-world aurora conditions. We do not collect your name or email address unless you choose to provide a display name.

2.4 Usage Analytics (Google Analytics)

We use Google Analytics to understand how users interact with the app. This collects anonymised data including pages visited, device type, approximate location (city level), and session duration. It does not personally identify you and is used only to improve the app.

You can opt out using the Google Analytics Opt-out Add-on or by enabling Do Not Track in your browser. Google processes this data in accordance with their Privacy Policy.

2.5 Embedded Live Camera: Queenstown (Roundshot)

⚠ Third-party content

The app includes a live camera feed for Queenstown, provided by Roundshot (roundshot.com). This is a third-party embed loaded from Roundshot's servers. When you view the live camera, Roundshot may set cookies on your device, collect your IP address, and log your access to their service. This data is collected by Roundshot, not by Spot The Aurora. We do not control or have access to it. See roundshot.com for their privacy practices. The camera can simply be ignored if you prefer not to interact with third-party embeds.

3. What We Do Not Collect

  • Your name or email address (unless you contact us directly)
  • Payment information. The app is completely free with no in-app purchases.
  • Account credentials. There is no login or account system.
  • Precise GPS coordinates stored on our servers
  • Any data used to personally identify you without your knowledge

4. How We Use Your Data

The limited data we collect is used only to:

  • Deliver localised aurora forecasts and space weather alerts
  • Send push notifications when you have opted in
  • Display community aurora sighting reports on the shared map
  • Understand and improve app performance through anonymised analytics

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, or any commercial purpose beyond operating Spot The Aurora.

5. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. Data is shared only in these limited circumstances:

  • Google Analytics: anonymised usage statistics as described in section 2.4
  • Roundshot: third-party live camera embed for Queenstown as described in section 2.5
  • Cloudflare: our hosting provider processes web traffic through their global network. See Cloudflare's Privacy Policy.
  • Legal obligations: we may disclose information if required by New Zealand law or a valid legal request

6. Data Storage and Security

Spot The Aurora is hosted on Cloudflare Pages with data processed through Cloudflare's global infrastructure, including servers in the United States. All data is transmitted over HTTPS. Push notification tokens and sighting report data are stored securely with access restricted to authorised operations only.

Aurora sighting reports are automatically deleted every 24 hours. Push notification tokens are stored indefinitely for as long as you have notifications enabled, and are only accessed by our own systems to send you alerts. No third party has access to your notification token. You can remove your token at any time by disabling notifications on your device.

7. International Data Transfers

As our infrastructure is hosted by Cloudflare and we use Google Analytics, your data may be processed on servers outside New Zealand, including in the United States. These transfers are made in accordance with applicable privacy laws. By using Spot The Aurora, you consent to your information being processed in these locations.

8. Your Rights (NZ Privacy Act 2020)

Under the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020, you have the right to:

  • Access personal information we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your information where applicable
  • Withdraw consent for push notifications at any time

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at help@spottheaurora.co.nz.

9. Children's Privacy

Spot The Aurora is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will remove it promptly.

10. Third-Party Data Sources

Spot The Aurora displays data from NOAA, NASA, ESA, and other public space weather agencies. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these organisations or any external links within the app.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we make significant changes, we will update the effective date at the top of this page. Continued use of Spot The Aurora after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
